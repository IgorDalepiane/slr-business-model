## RQ01: Business Model Identification and Taxonomy

**Question:**  
What business models have been identified in the literature for software product monetization, and how are they defined, classified, and differentiated from each other?

### Explanation

This question seeks a complete and systematic understanding of software business models, including:
- **Precise identification:** Names and formal definitions of models
- **Taxonomy:** Classification and categorization systems
- **Variations:** Subtypes and variations of each model
- **Differentiation:** How models distinguish themselves from each other

### Data Addressed
- **EQ01:** Name and definition of the business model
- **EQ02:** Synonyms and alternative terms used
- **EQ32:** Variations and subtypes of the model
- **Qualitative codes** related to model identification

### Justification

Establishing a solid conceptual foundation ensures that:
- All models are clearly identified and defined
- Terminology is standardized and understandable
- Variations and subtypes are adequately mapped
- Consistent comparative analyses are possible

### Example Data
Models identified include: Software-as-a-Service (SaaS), Freemium, Open Core, Marketplace, Platform Business Model, among others, each with definitions, synonyms, and documented variations.

## RQ03: Technical and Architectural Characteristics

**Question:**  
What are the technical, architectural, and delivery characteristics that distinguish different software business models, and how do these characteristics influence monetization?

### Explanation

Addresses how models work in practice:
- **Product/service types:** Application, Platform, API, SDK, Data, Managed Service, Infrastructure
- **Delivery modes:** On-premises, Cloud multi-tenant, Cloud single-tenant, Mobile, Desktop, Edge
- **Intellectual property:** Proprietary, Open Source, Dual License, Open Core
- **Licensing restrictions:** Technical compatibilities and limitations
- **Technical prerequisites:** Infrastructure and necessary technical conditions

### Data Addressed
- **EQ08:** Product/service type (Application 89.6%, Platform 79.1%)
- **EQ09:** Delivery/deployment (Multi-tenant SaaS 71.6%, Mobile 40.3%)
- **EQ10:** Intellectual property regime (Proprietary 37.3% when specified)
- **EQ11:** Licensing restrictions or compatibilities
- **EQ24:** Prerequisites and necessary conditions (technical and organizational aspects)

### Relationship with Monetization
- Multi-tenant SaaS (71.6%) enables economies of scale for low-cost subscriptions
- Open Source requires alternative monetization models (support, services, open core)
- Mobile (40.3%) facilitates freemium and in-app purchase models

## RQ04: Monetization and Pricing Strategies

**Question:**  
What are the monetization and pricing strategies used in different software business models, and how do these strategies relate to the characteristics of the model and the market?

### Explanation

Core of monetization, addressing:
- **Revenue sources:** Subscription (76.1%), Usage/consumption (41.8%), Transaction (32.8%), Advertising (19.4%), etc.
- **Pricing units:** User/seat (32.8%), Transactions (19.4%), Computing time (13.4%), etc.
- **Price structures:** Freemium (52.7%), Tiers/Plans (41.8%), Pay-as-you-go (41.8%), etc.
- **Currency and periodicity:** Operational billing aspects
- **Performance metrics:** Reported quantitative financial results

### Data Addressed
- **EQ12:** Revenue sources (Subscription dominant with 76.1%)
- **EQ13:** Base pricing unit (User/seat 32.8%)
- **EQ14:** Price presentation structure (Freemium 52.7%)
- **EQ15:** Currency and billing periodicity
- **EQ28:** Performance metrics and quantitative results
- **EQ20:** Business Model Canvas - Revenue Streams

### Identified Patterns
- **Subscription is dominant** (76.1%), especially in SaaS
- **Freemium is common** (52.7%) in B2C and mobile models
- **Multiple revenue sources** are frequent (Subscription + Usage combinations)
- **User/seat pricing** is most common unit (32.8%) for B2B

## RQ05: Ecosystem Dynamics and Go-to-Market Strategies

**Question:**  
What are the ecosystem dynamics (roles, relationships, network effects) and go-to-market strategies (acquisition channels, marketplaces) associated with different software business models?

### Explanation

Critical aspects of modern software models:
- **Ecosystem role:** Standalone product (49.3%), Two-sided platform (46.3%), Marketplace (13.4%)
- **Network effects:** Direct (35.8%), Cross-sided (31.3%), Data-driven (22.4%)
- **Acquisition channels:** Direct sales (47.8%), Product-led Growth (41.8%), Partners/channels (38.8%)
- **Marketplaces:** Apple App Store (20.9%), Google Play (19.4%), AWS Marketplace (3.0%)
- **Actors and interactions:** Mapping of ecosystem relationships

### Data Addressed
- **EQ16:** Company role in the ecosystem
- **EQ17:** Type of network effect
- **EQ18:** Customer acquisition channels
- **EQ19:** Use of marketplaces and platforms
- **EQ21:** Ecosystem actors and their interactions
- **EQ20:** Business Model Canvas - Channels, Relationships, Partnerships

### Expected Insights
- **Two-sided platforms** (46.3%) frequently have cross-sided network effects (31.3%)
- **App Stores** are dominant for B2C/mobile models (40.3%)
- **Product-led Growth** (41.8%) is growing, especially in SaaS
- **Partners/channels** (38.8%) still critical for Enterprise/B2B

## RQ10: Empirical Evidence and Study Methodologies

**Question:**  
What types of empirical evidence and research methodologies have been used to study software business models, and what are the patterns of available evidence for each model?

### Explanation

Fundamental for evaluating evidence quality:
- **Evidence types:** Single case study (20.9%), Multiple cases (50.7%), Survey (7.5%), Experiment (3.0%), Secondary data analysis (16.4%), Literature review (13.4%)
- **Quantitative metrics:** Reported numerical results (ARR, MRR, Churn, CAC, LTV, etc.)
- **Concrete examples:** Companies and products cited as examples
- **Limitations and gaps:** What is missing in available evidence
- **Methodological adequacy:** Which methods are most appropriate for each aspect

### Data Addressed
- **EQ30:** Type of evidence presented (Multiple cases 50.7% is dominant)
- **EQ28:** Reported metrics and quantitative results
- **EQ31:** Companies/products cited as examples
- **Methodological analysis** of articles included in the review

### Identified Evidence Patterns
- **Multiple cases dominate** (50.7%) - allow comparisons
- **Single case studies** (20.9%) - deep analysis of revealing cases
- **Experiments are rare** (3.0%) - opportunity for future research
- **Secondary data analysis** (16.4%) - growing data availability
